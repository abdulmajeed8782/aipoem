How to get Started on QuizoftheDay.net

You don’t need a manual or a tutorial. Just follow these four steps to build your quiz.

Step 1: Choose Number of Questions

Start by picking how many questions you want. The tool offers short and long formats 5, 10, 15, 20, or more. If you're short on time, go with fewer. If you're up for a challenge, pick more.

Step 2: Set the Difficulty Level

The tool offers four difficulty settings: Easy, Medium, Hard, and Expert.

Easy questions test basic knowledge and definitions. These work well for beginners or when you're learning a new subject. Think "What is the capital of France?" level questions.

Medium questions require some thinking and application. You need to understand concepts, not just memorize facts. Perfect for students who've covered the basics.

Hard questions demand deeper knowledge and critical thinking. They often involve multiple steps or require you to connect different concepts.

Expert questions are for people who know their stuff. These test advanced knowledge and problem-solving skills. If you're preparing for professional certifications, this is your level.

 

Step 3: Pick a Timer Option

You can set a timer for each question, the whole quiz, or go timer-free. If you're practicing for tests, timers help with time management. If you're just playing around, skip the clock.

Step 4: Start Quiz and Get Your Score

Once you're done answering, hit submit. The tool shows your score right away. You’ll see which answers were right, which were wrong, and your total points.

How the Scoring System Works?

When you finish a quiz, you see your score right away as a percentage. For example, 8 out of 10 means 80%.

You’ll also see which answers you got wrong and what the correct ones were. If you used a timer, quick and correct answers give you extra points but taking more time won’t hurt your score.

The quiz also shows how you did on easy or hard questions, so you know where to focus. Over time, you can track your progress and see how much you’ve improved.

No sugar-coating—just honest scores to help you get better.

Tips to Get the Most Out of Quiz of the Day

These strategies help you get better results from every quiz you take. Optimal Quiz Frequency Take quizzes daily, but keep them short rather than doing marathon sessions once a week.

1. Take Quizzes Daily

Short daily quizzes work better than long weekly ones. Think of it like exercising 10 questions a day to keep your brain active without stress. Mix topics throughout the week to stay fresh. For example, do math on Monday, science on Tuesday, and history on Wednesday.
Missed a day? No problem. Just getting back on track the next day consistency is what counts.

2. Start Easy, Then Level Up

Begin with a difficulty you’re comfortable with. When you consistently score 80% or more, move to the next level. Don’t skip steps, go from easy to medium, then hard.
If your score drops below 60%, go back a level. It’s better to build a strong base than rush ahead.

3. Use the Right Timer for Your Goal

Learning something new? Skip the timer and take your time. For regular practice, use the standard timer to build speed and focus. Preparing for a real test? Set a custom timer that matches the exam like 90 seconds per question.

4. Learn From Your Mistakes

The real learning happens after the quiz. Review every question you got wrong and read the correct answers carefully. Write down what you didn’t understand. Keep notes and go over them before your next quiz.
Notice any patterns in your mistakes are you rushing? Guessing? These clues help you improve. After some review, retake a similar quiz to test your progress.

The quiz ends when you see your score, but the learning starts when you review your mistakes.
